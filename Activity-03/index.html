Hong Kong Style Baked Pork Chop Rice

What You'll Need

  1. For The Marinade
    • Salt
    • Sugar
    • Pepper
    • Potato starch
    • Chicken bouillon powder
    • Light soy sauce
    • Egg
    • Sesame oil
    • Shaoxing rice wine
  1. For The Sauce
    • Tomatoes
    • Capsicums
    • Tomato paste
    • Suger
    • Salt
    • Onion
    • Tomato sauce/ Ketchup
    • Starch slurry
    • Garlic
  1. For The Grains
    • Pork chops
    • Rice
    • Eggs
    • Cheese

Cooking Steps

Step 1: Use the back of a knife to hammer at the meat until the whole surface is covered with lines. This will help to tenderize it.

Step 2: Preheat the oven to 180°C (356°F), then marinate the pork in the salt, sugar, chicken bouillon powder, pepper, Shaoxing wine, sesame oil, light soy sauce and 2 tbsp egg mix for a minimum of 20 minutes.

Step 3: Dice the tomatoes into 2cm (0.8″) chunks or to your preference.

Step 4: Repeat the chopping with the capsicums.

Step 5: Add 4 tbsp oil to the wok on medium heat and cook the onions and garlic for 3 minutes or until aromatic.

Step 6: Add the capsicums and stir fry on high heat for 30 seconds.

Step 7: Pour in the tomatoes and stir in for 1 minute.

Step 8: Add the tomato paste, tomato sauce, sugar and salt, then mix well.

Step 9: Let the sauce simmer on medium heat for 5 minutes and slowly pour in the mixed starch slurry as you stir until it has reached your preferred consistency. Set aside while you cook the other ingredients.

Step 10: Coat the meat in the remaining egg mix along with the corn starch.

Step 11: Pour enough oil in a wok to cover the surface and fry the pork chops on medium heat for 2 minutes on both sides, or until golden brown. Plate on paper towels to absorb the excess oil as you work on the other parts of the dish.

Step 12: Add 3 tbsp oil into a wok on medium heat, then turn to high and pour in the rice. Stir for 2 minutes and loosen the grains using your cooking utensil.

Beat the eggs and make a well in the center. Pour the mixture in and scramble it for 2 minutes or until mostly cooked.

Step 13: Mix the grains and eggs together and season with the salt and chicken bouillon powder.

Tip: For an even seasoning distribution, combine the salt and chicken bouillon powder with 4 tbsp water and use your fingers to dip and flick the water onto the grains.

Step 14: Evenly spread the fried rice out in an oven-proof dish and lay the pork on the top of the rice.

Step 15: Pour the tomato sauce over the meat and spread across until it covers everything underneath and top the dish with a layer of cheese.

Step 16: Bake the dish in the oven for 10 minutes or until the cheese is golden brown.

Serve the Baked Pork Chop Rice immediately as is!
