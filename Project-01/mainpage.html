Where is Hong Kong?

Introduction

Hong Kong situated along China's southern coast, comprises the main island of Hong Kong, nearby islets in the South China Sea, the bustling Kowloon Peninsula, and the expansive New Territories. Despite its return to Chinese sovereignty in 1997, Hong Kong retains a distinct identity as a Special Administrative Region, boasting a unique blend of tradition and modernity. This vibrant metropolis stands as one of Asia's most diverse and dynamic cities, where a cosmopolitan fusion of cultures thrives amidst its iconic skyline and bustling streets.

Nestled at the southern tip of the Pearl River Delta, Hong Kong embodies a rich tapestry of cultural influences shaped by centuries of migration and trade. Its culinary scene is renowned for its diversity, from humble street stalls offering mouthwatering dim sum to Michelin-starred restaurants showcasing innovative cuisine. Architecturally, Hong Kong is a captivating blend of old and new, with soaring skyscrapers juxtaposed against traditional Chinese temples and colonial-era buildings. Throughout the year, the city pulsates with festive energy, hosting a myriad of cultural events and celebrations that highlight its vibrant heritage.
